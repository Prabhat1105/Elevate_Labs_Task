Phishing Email Analysis Report

Elevate Labs Internship Task 2

1. Email Summary

  • Subject: Urgent: Verify Your Account
  • From: "Phishy Bank" <security@phishybank.com>
  • To: victim@example.com
  • Reply-To: "Phishy Support" <support@phishybank.com>
  • Date: Tue, 23 Sep 2025 12:08:06 +0530
  • Message-ID: <CAH9V34sK1m3YrBChg=6M6_dMXWb1iW7+9ja6WU_y3op_iuaDng@phishybank.com>

2. Header Analysis

  • DMARC: No DMARC record found for phishybank.com.
  • SPF: SPF authenticated for 192.0.2.123 (but not conclusive by itself).
  • DKIM: No DKIM-Signature present.
  • Received chain: 203.0.113.55 → mail.phishybank.com → mail.example.com (601 sec delay).
  • From vs Reply-To mismatch: security@phishybank.com vs support@phishybank.com.

3. Body / Content Indicators

  • Urgent language: "Verify your account immediately."
  • Suspicious URL (display vs actual):
    Visible: https://www.paypal.com/verify
    Actual: http://login.verify-now[.]ru/secure?id=ABC123
  • Greeting: Generic "Dear Customer".
  • Threatening tone: "Your account will be suspended within 24 hours."
  • Brand spoofing: Pretends to be PayPal.
  • Attachments: None in this sample.
  • Spelling / Grammar: Minor typos and awkward formatting.

4. URL & Domain Analysis

  • URL found: http://login.verify-now[.]ru/secure?id=ABC123
  • VirusTotal: Detected as malicious by several vendors.
  • urlscan.io: Shows redirects to maliciouscdn[.]xyz and fake PayPal login page.
  • WHOIS: Domain registered recently, privacy-protected → suspicious.

5. Phishing Traits Identified

  1. No DMARC / DKIM authentication.
  2. Reply-To mismatch.
  3. Suspicious / mismatched URLs.
  4. Urgent and threatening language.
  5. Brand impersonation (PayPal spoof).

6. Conclusion & Recommendations

Conclusion: This is a confirmed phishing attempt designed to steal credentials.

  • Delete and report the email.
  • Block domains/IPs: phishybank.com, login.verify-now.ru, 203.0.113.55, 192.0.2.123.
  • Implement DMARC, DKIM, SPF on legitimate domains.
  • Educate users to hover over links and enable MFA.
